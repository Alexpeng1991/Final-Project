Learn the Signs

What development milestones to look for?

All children have their unique time frame of developmental milestones (e.g., smiling, saying first words, notice the quantitative differences). However, a development that does not occur within an expected timeframe may indicate further assistance is needed for the child to excel his/her full potential

Why is it important

The influence of parental knowledge on a child’s development has been well established, and it is proven as a necessity for comprehensive cognitive enrichment for adjusting parental behavior or anticipating changes in children. For instance, parents who have knowledge on developmental milestones responds more sensitively their children's initiations.Studies have also demonstrated when parents have higher level knowledge on a child’s development; their children have higher cognitive skills and fewer behaviors problems
